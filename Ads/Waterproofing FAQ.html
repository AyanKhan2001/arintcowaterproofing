WATERPROOFING F.A.Q.

Information about waterproofing.  
What is it?  How it works? Which products?

What to know...

What is waterproofing?

Waterproofing is the creation of a barrier to stop water ingress to an item or area on the other side of the barrier.  This impervious product is normally flexible, will not easily crack, and has a very low water vapour transfer rate or MVTR.

Moisture resistant coatings such as paint or damp proofing is not waterproofing.  Some can be classified as moisture vapour control products, but they do not meet full specifications for a waterproofing claim.

Why waterproof?

Waterproofing will have structural protection properties and living space protection from excessive moisture and organic resulting effects such as mold and rot.

The two most common uses of waterproofing is to protect water ingress into living areas and to prevent rot and failure of walls. The other is to protect reinforcing steel in concrete structures.

If waterproofing is substituted for cheaper water resistant coatings or thinned out, expected results would be premature failure of the coating, humidity issues in living areas, loss of life in structural components it is protecting.

Typical product types.

Most waterproofing systems are liquid applied or sheet applied.  

Generally we find liquid applied systems easier to maintain quality control on site as immediate poor site conditions can be seen in liquid applied systems. Sheet systems can have great quality control and performance in the sheets themselves, but seams often have the same quality control issues on installations, this is their weak point. A common disadvantage of sheet membranes is once a hole is made in a sheet and if it is not fully bonded the location of the leak will be hard to find.

Roaron Construction will normally use a liquid hot rubber as it can have the best performance to cost ratio. However hot rubber is not UV stable and is easily damaged  In exposed locations, we may use other products which are UV stable such as a modified urethanes.  

Other products which offer different benefits are asphaltic (bitumen) sheet membranes, sprayable modified asphalt liquids, and hybrid urethanes. Each have different performance characteristics and pros, and cons. You must choose the right membrane for the susbstrate, condition of install, performance, climate and exposure.

What is flashing and reinforcing?

Flashing in a waterproofing system can sometimes be a rubberized sheet or reinforced fabric sheet embedded into the waterproofing system.  It does not neccessarily mean metal flashing.

Reinforcing is used over areas of future concern where movement may be possible. This reinforcement is a lightweight fabric which is embedded into the membrane system to help bridge and hold the membrane together should there be future movement.

What is drain board?

Drain board is commonly referred to as dimple board. There are different types and different strengths for each drain board manufactured. A drain board is used to provide water a clear path of escape (drainage) to a drain or off the membrane surface.

Drain boards are very common and help serve as an additional protection layer to the membrane.

What is protection board?

Membranes that are soft and highly flexible are readily damaged. A protection board is used as an effective method to protect the membrane and be compatible with the membrane.  The minimum thickness we recommend is 3mm.  The thickness of the protection system will often depend on the material and load placed on top.

During the application...

Does it smell?

Generally yes. Smells will be short lived. The stronger the smell, often the shorter the duration of the smell.  Some vapours may bother residents. It is important to understand the smell, it's possible consequence, and what to do to avoid the smell should it bother you before work starts.

Is it noisy?

Relatively no.  For hot rubber there will be the noise of a melter running.  This sound is similar to a generator running.  Most work during the membrane installation is usually low.  Preparation work prior to the membrane install will have noise depending on the type of preparation work needed.

Are there hazards?

Yes.  With all liquids there are hazards of slipping, flammables, airborn fumes, access restrictions.  Consult with us before you want to start work on what hazards may present themselves.

How long does it take to cool?

Hot applied product will cool almost immediately but it will remain sticky until dust and moisture are deposited on the surface.

Initial curing times for hot asphalt rubber or liquid rubber are about 5-10 minutes. Full curing of the membrane will take weeks.

Initial curing times for many urethanes is approximately 24 hours with final cure in approx 3 days.

More Tips and Answers

Signs the waterproofing has failed

There are numerous signs and the obvious one is leaking.  There are some other not so obvious signs too.  

Indications you have a failed waterproofing membrane include:
1. water ingress.
2. damp locations.
3. cold spots on the wall
4. mildew smell
5. white deposits on concrete that have dampness 
6. mold / mildew on wall, backside of wall, on insulation.
7. rotting wood
8. damp floors
9. high humidity (85%+)
10. rusting rebar in concrete.
11. water dripping through electrical conduits

How long does it last?

Most waterproofing applications have a life of 20+ years.  This is given the building isn't moving beyond the ability of the membrane to stretch.  Membranes that are buried and not exposed to weathering and the sun tend to last longer.

Can water be stopped from the inside?

Yes.  However, this is only done as a bandaid measure or a cost effective method to repair for convenience and lower cost.  The fix may not last as long and will not address the problem of a failed membrane.  

Generally, a membrane can not be placed on a surface to hold back water and be called waterproofing.

Warranties?

Yes.  We generally offer a 1 year warranty on repairs and up to 5 years for special systems.

Leaking at the bottom of the wall or through a floor?

First thing to do is to check your drain system.  Have a company inspect your perimeter drain and sump.  The lines should be clear with some residual soils often in them.  

Leaking near the bottom of the wall or through the floor often indicates a high water table due to plugged drains.

It's not wet but smells musty

High humidity is often due to high water tables or leaks. If you know the area is dry but it still smells musty, check the area with a humidity sensor. If the humidity is well above the next floor above, you may have a leak, or vapour transfer through the concrete. Sometimes we can address vapour transfer with special coatings.  Signs of vapour transfer can be white powder deposits on the floor or wall.
